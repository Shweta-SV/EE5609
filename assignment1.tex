\begin{flushleft}
\\ \bf Ques: Find a unit vector that makes an angle of 90\degree,135\degree,45\degree with positive X,Y and Z axis respectively.\end{flushleft}

\begin{flushleft}
 \textbf{Soln} : Given,
 
$\alpha =90\degree,

$\beta&=135\degree,

$\gamma&=45\degree$.

\end{flushleft}
\\

i.e l&=cos90\degree= 0,
m&=cos135\degree=\dfrac{-1}{\sqrt{2}},

n&=cos45\degree=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vspace{7mm}
\hspace{18mm}\
  
\begin{equation}
\implies\vec{m} = \myvec{\cos 90\degree     \\ \cos 135\degree\\ \cos45\degree}
\end{equation}
Also,we know that,

\begin{equation}

$\Vec{m}=\frac{\Vec{m}}{\lVert {m} \rVert}$
\vspace{12mm}

$\lVert \myvec{m} \rVert= \sqrt{0^2+(\frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}})^2+(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})^2} $
\\

\implies  {\lVert 
\myvec{m}
\rVert}=1\hspace{55mm}
\end{equation}\\

Hence,from (1)and(2) we have the unit vector: \\

$ \vec{m}=\myvec{ 0 \\  \frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}} \\ \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$
