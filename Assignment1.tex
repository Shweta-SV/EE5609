\\ \bf Ques: Find a unit vector that makes an angle of 90\degree,135\degree,45\degree with positive X,Y and Z axis respectively.\end{flushleft}
\begin{flushleft}
 \textbf{Soln} : Given,
 
$\alpha&=90\degree, 

$\beta&=135\degree,

$\gamma&=45\degree.\end{flushleft}


\\ 
i.e l=cos90\degree=0,
m=cos135\degree=\dfrac{-1}{\sqrt{2}},
n=cos45\degree=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\vspace{7mm}
\hspace{18mm}\
  
  \begin{equation}
      
  
  
   
\\\implies
   \bf \vec{m} =\begin{bmatrix} \myvec{\cos 90^{\circ} \\ \cos 135^{\circ} \\ \cos 45^{\circ}}\end{bmatrix}
   \hspace{64mm}  \end{equation} 
                      

\vspace{10mm}\\Also,we know that,

\begin{equation}

$\bf\Vec{m}=\frac{\Vec{m}}{\lVert {m}\rVert}$\vspace{8mm}

$\lVert \bf \myvec{m} \rVert$= \sqrt{0^2+(\frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}})^2+(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})^2} 
\vspace{8mm}
\\\implies\hspace{4mm} {\lVert \bf{m} \rVert}=1\hspace{77mm}\end{equation}\\

Hence,from (1)and(2) we have the unit vector: $\bf\vec{m}=\begin{bmatrix} \bf0 \\ \bf\frac{-1}{\sqrt{2}} \\ \bf\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\end{bmatrix}$
