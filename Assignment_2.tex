 \textbf{Soln} : 
\section{Problem}
If  
 \begin{align}
 \vec A = \myvec{\alpha&\beta\\\gamma&-\alpha},\
  \vec A^2 = I\label{eq1}
 \end{align}
choose the correct relation\\
(a)1+\alpha^2+\beta\gamma=0\ 
(b)1-\alpha^2+\beta\gamma=0 & \\
(c)1-\alpha^2-\beta\gamma=0\ 
(d)1-\alpha^2-\beta\gamma=0
\section{Solution}
\begin{align}
    \vec A^2 = \myvec{\alpha&\beta\\\gamma&-\alpha}
    \myvec{\alpha&\beta\\\gamma&-\alpha}= \myvec{\alpha^2+\beta\gamma&\alpha\beta-\alpha\beta\\\alpha\gamma -\alpha\gamma&\gamma\beta+\alpha^2}  
\end{align}
from \eqref{eq1}
\begin{align} 
\implies \myvec{\alpha^2+\beta\gamma&\alpha\beta-\alpha\beta\\\alpha\gamma -\alpha\gamma&\gamma\beta+\alpha^2}=\myvec{1&0\\0&1}\label{eq2}\end{align}
Therefore,on comparing \eqref{eq2}

\alpha^2+\beta\gamma = 1 

\implies 1-\alpha^2-\beta\gamma=0

Hence, (c) is the correct answer
